The ideas

Small rewrites, real impact.

Optimizations are not magic. They are provably equivalent rewrites that remove work before a program ever runs.

4+5

Constant folding

When every input is already known, calculate the result while compiling and remove the runtime operation completely.

add i32 4, 5  →  ret i32 9
×2ⁿ

Strength reduction

Replace a costly operation with an equivalent, simpler one. Multiplication by a positive power of two becomes a left shift.

mul i32 %x, 16  →  shl i32 %x, 4

Why it matters

Optimization is disciplined efficiency.

  • 1Less work at runtime. Constant folding turns a computation into a literal, which means no arithmetic instruction is executed for that expression.
  • 2Friendlier machine code. Shifts are a direct fit for multiplying by 2ⁿ and are a classic strength-reduction pattern.
  • 3Correctness comes first. The pass deliberately leaves unsupported cases alone. A “no change” result shows the guardrails that make optimization trustworthy.
